Energy Quantum Theory (EQT): A Unified Perspective on the Weak and Strong Forces

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Abstract: Core Concepts and Fundamental Propositions of the "Energy Quanta Theory" (EQT)

The "Energy Quanta Theory" (EQT) advocates using "frequency" and "energy density" as the primary language to describe nature, rather than a priori establishing "particles" or "fields" as fundamental entities. Key concepts include:

  • Energy Quantum (Energon): A primordial concept of a "discrete unit of energy" ($E = h\nu$), emphasizing an existence anchored by frequency.
  • Mass Quantum (Masson): A frequency-condensed stable state formed when energons become stationary through non-linear feedback and phase-locking.
  • Energy Quanta Density Field: Described by $\rho(\nu,x,t)$, where forces emerge from the gradient and time evolution of this density field.

Fundamental Propositions:

  1. On the Nature of Force: Force is a dynamic manifestation of the density field tending toward equilibrium: $f(x,t) \propto -\nabla\rho(x,t)$.
  2. On Mass Generation: Massons are formed by frequency condensation of high-frequency energons under critical density and coupling conditions.

EQT employs a "Phenomenon-Mechanism-Mathematics" Trinity construction, starting from observed phenomena to propose energons as the operational mechanism, formalized via density gradients.

Relationship with Traditional Concepts:
EQT reinterprets Standard Model bosons as energon aggregations within specific frequency bands. The Higgs Mechanism is viewed as a macroscopic expression of low-frequency condensation, while Gravity and Dark Energy are collective effects of the extremely low-frequency density field.
