Generative Ontology and Closure Dynamics (GOCD): A Background-Free Event Theory

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Generative Ontology with Closure Dynamics (GOCD)

This monograph develops a background-free generative framework—Generative Ontology with Closure Dynamics (GOCD)—aimed at rearticulating the foundational conditions under which physical facts, temporal order, and stability become possible.

Rejection of Primitive Assumptions:

GOCD rejects several assumptions commonly taken as primitive in contemporary physics:

  • ❌ Pre-given Spacetime
  • ❌ Continuous Dynamical Evolution
  • ❌ Ontic Probability
  • ❌ Object-based Ontology

The Generative Account of Existence

Instead, it proposes a generative account in which events, relations, closure, and freezing constitute the minimal elements of physical existence. Within this framework, existence is not treated as a metaphysical predicate or a state-based property, but as a decidable and fallible generative condition:

A structure exists IF AND ONLY IF a generative process achieves internal closure and subsequently enters a frozen, reusable role in further generation.

Systematic Reconstructions

The work offers systematic reconstructions of time, causality, probability, continuity, and stability, showing them to be outcomes of generative constraints and readout protocols rather than primitive features of the world.

Special emphasis is placed on failure conditions, under which the framework must be abandoned if closure, freezing, or readout coherence cannot be realized.

Foundational Intent

This work is not intended as a phenomenological model, a modification of existing physical theories, or an immediately predictive framework. Rather, it serves as a foundational proposal addressing the conditions of possibility for physical theory itself.
