Generative Ontology and Closure Dynamics (GOCD): A Background-Free Event Theory

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Abstract: This monograph proposes Generative Ontology with Closure Dynamics (GOCD), a foundational framework that reconstructs physical existence without presupposing spacetime, continuous dynamics, or object-based metaphysics.

The central claim of GOCD is that existence is not a primitive predicate, but a fallible generative achievement.

A structure is recognized as "existing" only if it satisfies two rigorous conditions:

  • Internal Closure: The generative process must satisfy a specific closure condition.
  • Frozen Role: The structure must enter a stable, "frozen" state that allows it to participate coherently in subsequent generations.

On this basis, core physical concepts are redefined as structured outcomes of generative constraints:

Temporal Arrow: Derived from the monotonic accumulation of frozen structures, not entropy.
Causality: Readout protocols operating under finite resolution.

Crucially, GOCD treats falsifiability not as an external criterion, but as an internal structural requirement. The theory must be rejected if:

  1. Closure cannot be achieved at any scale.
  2. Freezing fails to yield reusable structures.
  3. Increased readout fidelity fails to modulate statistical behavior.

GOCD provides a minimal generative grammar to clarify what it means for physical facts to occur, persist, and fail.
