The Quantum Energy Universe: A Unified Theory of Gravity, Dark Matter, and Dark Energy

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Abstract: Confronted with the mysteries of dark matter, dark energy, and the unification of gravity, EQT introduces a theory based on energy quantum density gradients. It posits that all fundamental interactions arise from energy minimization driven by quantum exchange, linking microscopic behavior to macroscopic cosmic structure through positive feedback mechanisms.

The Frequency Spectrum of the Dark Universe

This theory provides a quantifiable microscopic mechanism by categorizing non-baryonic phenomena into specific frequency domains:

Component Frequency Range ($f$) Energy ($E$)
Dark Matter $10^{3} < f < 10^{10}$ Hz $10^{-31}$ to $10^{-24}$ J
Dark Energy $10^{-33} < f < 10^{-4}$ Hz $10^{-67}$ to $10^{-38}$ J

Theoretical Advantages & Testability

The EQT density gradient theory offers a clear path toward experimental validation:

  • Unification: Bridging microscopic particle interactions and macroscopic cosmic structures via density gradients ($\nabla\rho$).
  • N-Body Simulations: Providing mathematical models verifiable through numerical simulations of galactic formation.
  • Experimental Alignment:
    • Dark Matter: Mid-frequency signals ($\approx 10$ Hz) detectable via Fermi-LAT axion searches.
    • Dark Energy: Ultra-low-frequency backgrounds ($\approx 10^{-18}$ Hz) alignable with LISA gravitational wave observations.

This monograph systematically elaborates these derivations and numerical simulations, offering a new unified perspective to unravel the ultimate fate of the cosmos.
