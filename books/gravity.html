Gravitational Unification: A Theory of Cosmic Evolution Based on Energy Quantum Density Gradients

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Abstract: Where do the mysteries of the universe originate? From the fiery inception of the Big Bang to the majestic spirals of galaxies, from the subtle bonding of molecular structures to the complex emergence of life, the universe challenges human understanding with its profound laws and boundless diversity. Traditional physics, through the Standard Model and General Relativity, has elucidated the four fundamental interactions (gravity, electromagnetism, strong nuclear force, and weak nuclear force), yet struggles to unify these forces and uncover the origins of complexity. The Energy Quantum Hypothesis emerges as a bold and unifying theoretical framework: by classifying interactions through frequency (high-frequency electromagnetic, low-frequency gravitational, ultra-high-frequency gluons) and dynamics of density fields, it integrates the four fundamental interactions, dark energy, dark matter, and even the origins of life into a single mechanism—the dynamic evolution of energy quantum exchange. This hypothesis not only redefines the boundaries of physics but also provides interdisciplinary inspiration for cosmology, complexity science, astrobiology, and the philosophy of science. Core Concept of the Energy Quantum Hypothesis The Energy Quantum Hypothesis centers on the concept of "energy quanta," redefining the mediator particles of fundamental interactions (e.g., photons, gravitons, gluons, W/Z bosons) as energy quanta, described uniformly through frequency classification and the dynamic evolution of a density field . The dynamic equation describes the evolution of the density field, where the positive feedback term drives the formation of high-density regions, the diffusion term simulates energy dissipation, the fluid term describes material convection, and the quantum correction term introduces random fluctuations. Forces arise from density gradients.Frequency classification divides energy quanta into:High-Frequency Energy Quanta f > 10^{10Hz, e.g., photons, driving electromagnetism, energy E =10^{-5}-10eV;Low-Frequency Energy Quantaf <10^3Hz, e.g., gravitons, driving gravity, E=10^{-21}eV;Ultra-High-Frequency Energy Quanta f =10^{20}Hz, e.g., gluons, driving the strong nuclear force, E=10^8eV; Weak Nuclear Force Energy Quanta(W/Z bosons, frequency TBD, f = 10^{25}Hz}. This framework unifies the four fundamental interactions as cross-scale processes of energy quantum exchange, connecting microscopic randomness with macroscopic order . Theoretical Contributions: Unification and Cross-Scale Breakthroughs The Energy Quantum Hypothesis achieves theoretical breakthroughs in the following areas: Mathematical Framework for Unifying the Four Forces: Through density fields and frequency classification, the hypothesis unifies gravity (low-frequency), electromagnetism (high-frequency), and the strong nuclear force (ultra-high-frequency), with forces driven by density gradients, and nuclear force potentials. The Lagrangian describes photon-graviton coupling, with attempts to incorporate the weak nuclear force. Explanation of Dark Energy and Dark Matter: Dark energy is conceptualized as a uniform background of ultra-low-frequency energy quanta, driving cosmic acceleration; dark matter is attributed to high-density regions of mid-frequency energy quanta, enhancing gravitational effects . Mechanism for Complexity Emergence: Positive feedback and quantum fluctuations drive cross-scale structure formation from molecular bonds (electromagnetism) to galaxies (gravity), akin to dissipative structures (Prigogine, 1984), explaining the origins of life and self-organization in complex systems. Unification of Positive and Negative Polarities}: Positive and negative charges (electromagnetism) and positive/negative graviton polarities (weak repulsive effects) are interpreted as high/low-density regions of the density field, unifying the microscopic mechanisms of interactions.
