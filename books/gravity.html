Gravitational Unification: A Theory of Cosmic Evolution Based on Energy Quantum Density Gradients

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Abstract: Where do the mysteries of the universe originate? From the fiery inception of the Big Bang to the complex emergence of life, the universe challenges us to find a single, unifying law. While traditional physics struggles to bridge the Standard Model and General Relativity, the Energy Quantum Hypothesis emerges as a bold framework to integrate the four fundamental interactions, dark energy, and complexity science into a single mechanism: the dynamic evolution of energy quantum exchange.

Core Concept: Frequency Classification

The hypothesis redefines mediator particles (photons, gravitons, gluons) as energy quanta, categorized by their operational frequency ($f$):

  • Ultra-High-Frequency ($f \approx 10^{20}$ Hz): Gluons/Strong Nuclear Force ($E \approx 10^{8}$ eV).
  • High-Frequency ($f > 10^{10}$ Hz): Photons/Electromagnetism ($E \approx 10^{-5} - 10$ eV).
  • Low-Frequency ($f < 10^{3}$ Hz): Gravitons/Gravity ($E \approx 10^{-21}$ eV).
  • Weak Nuclear Force ($f \approx 10^{25}$ Hz): W/Z Bosons (Under refinement).

Theoretical Contributions

1. Unification of the Four Forces: Forces are not independent mediators but manifestations of density gradients within the energy quantum field. This connects microscopic randomness with macroscopic order through a unified Lagrangian framework.

2. Dark Energy & Dark Matter:

  • Dark Energy: A uniform background of ultra-low-frequency quanta driving cosmic acceleration.
  • Dark Matter: High-density regions of mid-frequency quanta enhancing gravitational effects.

3. Complexity & Life: By applying positive feedback and quantum fluctuations, the theory explains cross-scale structure formation—from molecular bonds to galaxies—consistent with dissipative structure theory (Prigogine, 1984).

4. Polarity Unification: Positive/negative charges and gravitational polarities are reinterpreted as high/low-density fluctuations of the same underlying field.

This framework provides a direct causal chain between observed phenomena and mathematical structures, redefining the boundaries of physics and philosophy.
