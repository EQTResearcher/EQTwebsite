Gravitational Unification: A Theory of Cosmic Evolution Based on Energy Quantum Density Gradients

Authors: Kaisheng Li, Longji Li

Status: Available / In Press

Where do the mysteries of the universe originate? From the fiery inception of the Big Bang to the majestic spirals of galaxies, from the subtle bonding of molecular structures to the complex emergence of life, the universe challenges human understanding with its profound laws and boundless diversity.

Traditional physics, through the Standard Model and General Relativity, has elucidated the four fundamental interactions (gravity, electromagnetism, strong nuclear force, and weak nuclear force), yet struggles to unify these forces and uncover the origins of complexity. The Energy Quantum Hypothesis emerges as a bold and unifying theoretical framework: by classifying interactions through frequency and dynamics of density fields, it integrates the four fundamental interactions, dark energy, dark matter, and even the origins of life into a single mechanism—the dynamic evolution of energy quantum exchange.

Core Concept of the Energy Quantum Hypothesis

The hypothesis centers on redefining mediator particles as energy quanta, described uniformly through frequency classification and the dynamic evolution of a density field. The dynamic equation incorporates:

  • Positive Feedback: Drives the formation of high-density regions.
  • Diffusion Term: Simulates energy dissipation.
  • Fluid Term: Describes material convection.
  • Quantum Correction: Introduces random fluctuations.

Theoretical Contributions: Unification and Cross-Scale Breakthroughs

The Energy Quantum Hypothesis achieves theoretical breakthroughs in the following areas:

1. Mathematical Framework for Unifying the Four Forces:

Through density fields and frequency classification, the hypothesis unifies gravity, electromagnetism, and the strong nuclear force, with forces driven by density gradients and nuclear force potentials. The Lagrangian describes photon-graviton coupling.

2. Explanation of Dark Energy and Dark Matter:

Dark energy is conceptualized as a uniform background of ultra-low-frequency energy quanta; dark matter is attributed to high-density regions of mid-frequency energy quanta, enhancing gravitational effects.

3. Mechanism for Complexity Emergence:

Positive feedback and quantum fluctuations drive cross-scale structure formation akin to dissipative structures (Prigogine, 1984), explaining the origins of life and self-organization in complex systems.

4. Unification of Positive and Negative Polarities:

Positive and negative charges and graviton polarities are interpreted as high/low-density regions of the density field, unifying the microscopic mechanisms of interactions.

This framework unifies the four fundamental interactions as cross-scale processes of energy quantum exchange, connecting microscopic randomness with macroscopic order.
